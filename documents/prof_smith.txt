Professor Smith
Course: CS101 - Introduction to Programming

Students generally describe Professor Smith as beginner-friendly and approachable. Lectures move at a slower pace than most CS courses.

Several students mention that attendance helps because many exam questions are similar to examples shown during class.

Homework assignments are not especially difficult but can take longer for students who have never programmed before.

Office hours are popular and often crowded before exams.

Many reviews mention that Professor Smith gives detailed feedback on projects and coding assignments.

Students recommend this course for beginners who want a supportive introduction to programming.
